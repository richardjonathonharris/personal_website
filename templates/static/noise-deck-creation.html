I'm starting this project with the Anarch runner Ji "Noise" Reilly. Not only is he the first ID in the core set, but how could you say no to that face?

Noise was the first Anarch available to runners. His ability revolves around forcing the opponent to discard cards off of the top of their deck after a Virus program is installed. This can present a fairly strong challenge to the Corporation, but not an insurmountable one (particularly with the release of Jackson Howards). Typically, a Noise player is looking to install (and install, and install) viruses, thereby disprupting the Corporation's plans. He's a fairly popular ID, with over 1,200 decks posted to NetrunnerDB as of February 1, 2016.

{% include "bar.html" %}

There are certainly some changes month to month (Spring 2015 seems especially tough for poor Noise) but on average, there's roughly 50 new decks posted a month to NetrunnerDB. That's not too shabby! Given how long he's been in play, we'd expect to see a lot of decks and a lot of different archetypes that take advantage of new support cards that help him indirectly, or new viruses that help him directly. I plot out all the decks from December 2013 to February 2016. For this analysis, I've gone ahead with 6 clusters this time, but there's certainly an argument to be made for one or two fewere (or more).
